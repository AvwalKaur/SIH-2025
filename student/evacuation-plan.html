Emergency Evacuation Plan

Introduction

This Emergency Evacuation Plan has been developed to ensure the safe and orderly evacuation of all occupants from IGDTUW buildings in the event of an emergency. The plan outlines procedures, responsibilities, and assembly points to minimize risk to life and property.

Important: All students, staff, and visitors must familiarize themselves with this evacuation plan and follow instructions during emergencies.

Emergency Procedures

General Evacuation Procedure

  1. Remain calm. Do not panic.
  2. If you discover a fire, activate the nearest fire alarm.
  3. Evacuate the building immediately using the nearest safe exit.
  4. Do not use elevators during a fire emergency.
  5. Assist persons with disabilities in evacuation.
  6. Proceed directly to your designated assembly area.
  7. Do not re-enter the building until authorized by emergency personnel.

Specific Emergency Procedures

Fire Emergency

  • Activate nearest fire alarm
  • Use stairs, not elevators
  • Close doors behind you
  • If trapped, signal from window
  • Use fire extinguisher only if trained

Earthquake

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • Stay away from windows
  • Evacuate after shaking stops
  • Watch for falling debris
  • Assemble in open areas

Medical Emergency

  • Call University Health Center
  • Provide first aid if trained
  • Do not move seriously injured
  • Clear area for emergency personnel
  • Remain with person until help arrives

Evacuation for Persons with Disabilities

Persons with mobility impairments should proceed to designated Areas of Refuge (stairwell landings) and wait for assistance. Evacuation chairs are available at strategic locations. Inform security or evacuation wardens about your location.

Roles and Responsibilities

Evacuation Wardens

Designated staff members who assist in evacuation:

  • Ensure all occupants evacuate the building
  • Check rooms and areas under their responsibility
  • Assist persons with disabilities
  • Report status to Emergency Coordinator

Department Heads/Faculty

  • Ensure evacuation of students/staff in their area
  • Account for persons at assembly points
  • Report missing persons to emergency personnel

Security Personnel

  • Direct evacuation flow
  • Secure building perimeter
  • Assist emergency services
  • Prevent re-entry until safe

Training and Drills

Regular evacuation drills will be conducted to ensure all occupants are familiar with evacuation procedures. Participation in these drills is mandatory for all students and staff.

Schedule

  • Fire drills: Twice per year
  • Earthquake drills: Once per year
  • Special scenario drills: As needed

All new students and staff will receive orientation on emergency procedures during induction programs.
